Clinical Question

Is MRD negativity a valid surrogate for overall survival in randomized trials of acute myeloid leukaemia?

Measurable residual disease (MRD) has emerged as a powerful prognostic marker in AML, yet its value as a surrogate endpoint in randomized controlled trials remains unestablished. A trial-level surrogacy analysis quantifies the correlation between treatment effects on MRD — and on CR and EFS — with the treatment effect on overall survival, using the weighted regression framework of Buyse and Burzykowski.

PICO Framework
Structured research question
P
Population
Adults (≥18 years) with newly diagnosed AML enrolled in randomized controlled trials, regardless of cytogenetic or molecular risk. Both intensive and non-intensive treatment settings included.
I
Intervention
Any experimental intervention: FLT3 inhibitors, IDH1/2 inhibitors, venetoclax combinations, anti-CD33 / GO, immunotherapy, hypomethylating agents, or chemotherapy intensification strategies.
C
Comparator
Standard of care arm: placebo plus chemotherapy, best supportive care, or physician's choice, as defined by each trial.
O
Outcomes
Primary: Trial-level association between treatment effect on MRD negativity (log OR) and OS (log HR) by weighted least-squares regression.

Secondary: Analogous associations for CR vs OS and EFS vs OS. β coefficient, R², p-value, and 95% CI reported per surrogate.
Analytical Framework
Study Design

Systematic review of Phase II–III RCTs in newly diagnosed AML. Each trial arm contributes one point to the regression. Database updated continuously as new results are published.

Statistical Method

Weighted least-squares (WLS) regression at the trial level. Each point weighted by 1/SE²(OS HR), giving larger and more precise trials greater influence on the regression line.

Surrogates Evaluated

MRD negativity rate (log OR from counts + 0.5 continuity correction), CR/CRi rate (log OR from counts), and EFS (log HR from reported HR and 95% CI). Separate regression models.

Living Review

Data in a version-controlled JSON file on GitHub. One row per trial arm. New trials are added as published; graphs and statistics update on the next page load automatically.
